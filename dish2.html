Rasmalai

Rasmalai is yet another one of my favorite sweets. This Bengali specialty is made with rasgulla (sweetened balls of paneer) The result is a creamy, dreamy dessert that absolutely melts in your mouth.



    Ingredients:


  • 4 cup milk, full cream
  • ½ tsp cardamom powder
  • few threads saffron / kesar
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp dry fruits, chopped


    Directions:


  1. firstly, prepare the rabdi by boiling 4 cup milk.
  2. stir occasionally, to prevent milk from burning at the bottom.
  3. now add ½ tsp cardamom powder and few threads saffron.
  4. also add ¼ cup sugar. add more sugar if required.
  5. stir well and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. scrape off the cream from sides and allow milk to thicken.
  7. add in 2 tbsp dry fruits like cashew, almonds and pistachios.
  8. simmer for another 5 minutes stirring occasionally.
  9. finally, the rabri is ready once the milk thickens. keep aside.
  10. prepare the rasmalai from milk powder by taking 1 cup milk powder in a large kadai.
  11. add in 2 tbsp powdered sugar and ¼ cup milk.
  12. keeping the flame on low, stir continuously till the milk combines well with milk powder.
  13. scrape from bottom till the milk thickens and no lumps present.
  14. now add ½ tsp ghee and mix well.
  15. mix till the mixture starts to separate from the pan and forms a lump.
  16. further, grease hands with ghee and prepare small balls and flatten.
  17. finally, pour rabdi and serve instant milk powder rasmalai.
